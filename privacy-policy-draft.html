1. Who We Are

Housman Learning Academy (academy.housmanlearning.com) is the professional development platform operated by Housman Institute, LLC, located in Newton, Massachusetts. We provide online courses, coaching, and program support for early childhood educators, mentors, school leaders, and administrators.

This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect through the Housman Learning Academy platform, how we use it, and what choices you have. It applies only to this platform — our separate e-commerce website (housmanlearning.com) has its own privacy practices.

2. Information We Collect

Account Information

When your account is created, we collect:

  • Full name
  • Email address
  • Institutional affiliation (school, district, or organization)
  • Role (teacher, mentor, school leader, coach, or administrator)

Course and Program Activity

As you use the platform, we collect information about your learning activity, including:

  • Course enrollments, progress, and completion status
  • Quiz and assessment responses and scores
  • Reflective practice journal entries and session notes
  • Coaching session records and feedback
  • Classroom visit observation data (for the B2E Mastery Program)
  • Certificates earned

De-Identified Student Data (B2E Mastery Program Only)

For participants in the B2E Mastery Program, school districts provide de-identified student data — meaning student ID codes, not student names — for the purpose of program evaluation and research. This data cannot be linked to an identifiable child by Housman. We explain this in more detail in Section 7 below.

Technical and Usage Data

When you visit or use the platform, we automatically collect:

  • IP address
  • Browser type and version
  • Device type and operating system
  • Pages visited and time spent on each page
  • Referring website
  • Click patterns and scrolling behavior (via session recording tools)

3. How We Collect Information

Directly from You

You provide information when you log in, complete coursework, submit assessments, write reflections, or interact with your coach through the platform.

From Your Institution

Most users access Housman Learning Academy through an institutional partnership. Your school district or organization provides us with your name, email, and role so we can set up your account and enroll you in the appropriate program. For the B2E Mastery Program, institutions also provide de-identified student data for program evaluation.

Automatically Through Technology

We use cookies and similar tracking technologies to collect technical and usage data when you visit the platform. See Section 5 for details.

4. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect to:

  • Deliver our programs — manage your account, track your course progress, facilitate coaching sessions, and issue certificates
  • Support your learning — enable coaches to provide personalized feedback, track assessment results, and guide your professional development
  • Report to your institution — share progress and completion data with the school district or organization that enrolled you, as part of our program agreements with them
  • Evaluate and improve our programs — analyze course completion rates, assessment outcomes, and user feedback to make our programs more effective
  • Conduct research — use de-identified and aggregated data to study the effectiveness of social-emotional learning approaches in early childhood education
  • Maintain and secure the platform — monitor for technical issues, prevent unauthorized access, and keep the platform running smoothly
  • Communicate with you — send program-related notifications, coaching reminders, and important platform updates

5. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

We use cookies and similar technologies to operate the platform and understand how it is used. Here is what is active on the site:

Essential Cookies

  • WordPress session cookies — keep you logged in and manage your session. These are necessary for the platform to function.
  • LearnDash and BuddyBoss cookies — support our learning management system and community features. Required for course delivery and platform functionality.

Analytics and Performance

  • Google Analytics — helps us understand how users navigate the platform, which pages are most visited, and where users may encounter difficulties. Data is aggregated and used to improve the user experience.
  • Google Tag Manager — manages the loading of our analytics and tracking scripts. It does not collect personal data on its own but coordinates the other tools listed here.
  • Microsoft Clarity — records user sessions (clicks, scrolling, mouse movement) and generates heatmaps so we can see how people interact with the platform. This helps us identify usability issues and improve page layouts. Clarity masks sensitive input fields by default.

Communication and Support

  • Zoho SalesIQ — powers the chat widget on our login page (the only public-facing page on the platform) and tracks visitor activity on that page. This allows us to offer real-time support to users who need help logging in.
  • Zoho Forms — processes form submissions on the platform. Data submitted through Zoho Forms is stored on Zoho's servers and transferred to our systems.

Your Choices

You can manage cookies and tracking in the following ways:

  • Browser settings — most web browsers allow you to block or delete cookies through their settings. Be aware that disabling essential cookies will prevent you from logging in and using the platform.
  • Google Analytics opt-out — you can install the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on to prevent Google Analytics from collecting your data.
  • Microsoft Clarity opt-out — you can opt out of Clarity data collection through your browser's Do Not Track setting or by blocking clarity.ms in your browser.

Do Not Track Signals

Some browsers offer a "Do Not Track" (DNT) setting. There is currently no universal standard for how websites should respond to DNT signals. We do not currently change our practices in response to DNT signals, but we respect your right to use browser-level controls described above to manage tracking.

6. How We Share Your Information

We do not sell your personal information. We share data only in the following circumstances:

With Your Institution

If you are enrolled through a school district or organization, we share your course progress, completion status, assessment results, and coaching records with authorized representatives of that institution. This is a core part of our program agreements — your institution has engaged us to provide professional development and needs to track participation and outcomes.

With Coaches

Housman Learning coaches access your program progress, session notes, classroom visit observations, and assessment data in order to provide you with personalized support and feedback. Coaches may work directly for Housman or be designated by your institution.

With Service Providers

We use third-party services to operate the platform, including:

  • Amazon Web Services (AWS) — cloud hosting and infrastructure
  • Google (Analytics, Tag Manager) — usage analytics
  • Microsoft Clarity — session recording and heatmaps
  • Zoho (SalesIQ, Forms) — chat support and form processing

These providers process data on our behalf and are contractually required to use it only for the services they provide to us.

When Required by Law

We may disclose your information if required to do so by law, regulation, legal process, or enforceable governmental request.

With Your Consent

We may share your information in other situations with your explicit consent.

7. De-Identified Student Data (B2E Mastery Program)

This section applies only to participants in the B2E Mastery Program and the school districts that partner with us for that program.

What We Receive

As part of the B2E Mastery Program, participating school districts provide us with de-identified student data for program evaluation and research. This means districts share student ID codes — not student names, dates of birth, or other directly identifying information. We cannot link this data to any identifiable child.

How It Is Used

This de-identified data is used to evaluate the effectiveness of the B2E Mastery Program and to conduct research on social-emotional learning outcomes in early childhood settings. It is never used for marketing, advertising, or any purpose unrelated to program evaluation and research.

Alignment with Federal Education Privacy Principles

Because the student data we receive is de-identified and cannot be linked to individual children, it does not constitute "education records" under the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA). Nonetheless, we treat all student-related data with the same care and restrict its use to program evaluation and research purposes only.

Children Never Access This Platform

Housman Learning Academy is designed exclusively for adult educators. Children do not create accounts, log in, or interact with the platform in any way. The de-identified student data we receive is provided by school districts, not collected from children or their parents.

Our Other Products

Our Short Courses and ECSELent Adventures products do not involve any student or child data.

8. Data Retention

We retain your account and program data for as long as needed to fulfill the purposes described in this policy. Specifically:

  • Account information — retained for the duration of your enrollment and for a reasonable period afterward to support certificate verification and institutional reporting obligations
  • Course progress and completion records — retained to maintain your learning history and to support ongoing institutional program reporting
  • Coaching and assessment records — retained for the duration of your program participation and the institutional reporting period
  • De-identified student data — retained for as long as needed for program evaluation and research, consistent with our agreements with participating school districts
  • Technical and usage data — retained according to the default retention periods of the respective analytics tools (typically 14 to 26 months)

When data is no longer needed, we delete or anonymize it. If your institution's partnership with us ends, we work with the institution to determine appropriate data disposition.

9. Your Rights

Depending on where you live, you may have certain rights regarding your personal information. These may include the right to:

  • Access your personal information — request a copy of the data we hold about you
  • Correct inaccurate information — ask us to fix errors in your data
  • Delete your personal information — request that we remove your data, subject to any legal or contractual obligations we have to retain it
  • Opt out of analytics tracking — you can disable non-essential cookies through your browser settings

If your account was created through an institutional partnership, some requests may need to be coordinated with your school district or organization, as they are the entity that engaged us to process your data.

To exercise any of these rights, contact us using the information in Section 13 below. We will respond to your request within 30 days.

10. Security

We take the security of your information seriously. Our measures include:

  • Encrypted connections (HTTPS/TLS) for all data transmitted to and from the platform
  • Secure cloud hosting on Amazon Web Services with access controls and monitoring
  • Role-based access controls — users only see the data relevant to their role
  • Regular security reviews and updates to our platform and infrastructure
  • Audit logging of administrative actions and data access

No system is perfectly secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security. If you become aware of any unauthorized access to your account, please contact us immediately.

Data Breach Notification

In the event of a data breach that compromises your personal information, we will notify affected users and the appropriate authorities in accordance with Massachusetts law and any other applicable regulations. We will provide notification as promptly as reasonably possible and will include information about what occurred, what data was affected, and what steps you can take.
